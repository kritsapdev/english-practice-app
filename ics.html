(S1) Good morning, distinguished committee members, honored guests, and all attendees.
(S2) My name is Krisana Sapsinchai, from the Thai-Nichi Institute of Technology.
(S3) Today, I am honored to present my research on the topic
(S4) A Retrieval-Augmented Generation System for a Legal Advisory System in Thai Consumer Protection.
(S5) The e-commerce market in Thailand has experienced exponential growth in recent years
(S6) The retail and wholesale sector's value increased from 2.5 trillion baht in 2021 to over 2.8 trillion baht in 2023
(S7) However, this rapid growth has led to a corresponding increase in consumer complaints
(S8) particularly concerning exploitation by online sellers
(S9) Unfortunately, accessing legal experts is often costly and time-consuming,
(S10) leaving many consumers without the protection they rightfully deserve
(S11) Therefore, this research aims to develop a preliminary advisory system
(S12) for Thai consumer protection law to serve as a guide and bridge this accessibility gap.
(S13) Our system utilizes Generative AI technology
(S14) with its core being a technique called Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)
(S15) This approach is fundamentally different from the conventional fine-tuning of Large Language Models (LLMs).
(S16) Why RAG?. The primary advantage of RAG. is its ability. to mitigate the problem of Hallucination.
(S17) where the AI generates incorrect. or fabricated information.
(S18) The system first "Retrieves" information from a curated Knowledge Base we have prepared
(S19) Then, it "Generates" an answer based exclusively on the retrieved data.
(S20) This ensures that the answers are reliable and legally accurate.
(S21) Furthermore, if there are new legal updates, they can be quickly integrated into the system.
(S22) Our process began with Dataset Collection. We gathered data from two primary sources
(S23) Official Legal Documents: Including acts such as the Consumer Protection Act, the Drug Act, and the Food Act
(S24) sourced from the website of the Office of the Consumer Protection Board (OCPB). A total of 11 acts were collected.
(S25) Articles and Case Studies: From the Thailand Consumer Council, we collected 47 articles to gain insights into real-world applications and consumer issues.
(S26) In this research, we introduce Semantic Legal Preprocessing (SLP). This is our core solution to the limitations of traditional RAG
(S27) which often relies on simplistic, fixed-size chunking based on token count or character length.
(S28) In a legal context, this method is highly inadequate, as arbitrary splits can disrupt the continuity of statutes, definitions, and complex legal arguments,
(S29) thereby destroying semantic integrity and hindering accurate reasoning.
(S30) The SLP process involves feeding the raw dataset,
(S31) along with specialized Prompt Engineering, into ChatGPT-4o.
(S32) This transforms complex Thai legal documents into a structured Question-Answer-Explanation-Sample (Q-A-E-S) format.
(S33) The Q-A-E-S structure ensures that each vector entry is a complete
(S34) self-contained unit of legal knowledge. This significantly improves retrieval quality and the coherence of the generated responses.
(S35) The outcome of this process is a semantically rich dataset ready for use, comprising a total of 2,000 rows.
(S36) Next, we convert the data processed by SLP into numerical vectors using an Embedding Model.
(S37) This data is then stored in a private cloud vector database service,
(S38) enabling rapid retrieval of information that is semantically similar to the user's query.
(S39) For this research, we selected and tested six different Embedding Models.
(S40) Each model supports the Thai language and is capable of handling a high number of tokens.
(S41) Our evaluation was conducted in two parts: Embedding Model Evaluation and Generation Model Evaluation.
(S42) For the Embedding Model Evaluation, we tested the six models using standard metrics
(S43) Mean Reciprocal Rank (MRR), Hit Rate, and Precision, against 25% of the questions from our database.
(S44) The results showed that the BGE-M3 model achieved the highest MRR score of 0.54,
(S45) indicating its effectiveness in ranking the correct answer near the top. Meanwhile,
(S46) the WangchanX-Legal-ThaiCCL-Retriever model scored the highest on both Hit Rate and Precision at 0.71,
(S47) demonstrating its superior accuracy in retrieving relevant information.
(S48) For the generation part of the system, we selected three Small Language Models (SLMs) for testing.
(S49) These models support the Thai language and are computationally efficient due to their smaller size.
(S50) For the Generation phase, we paired the best-performing embedding model with three Small Language Models (SLMs):
(S51) Llama3.2, Typhoon, and OpenThaiGPT. We evaluated their performance using the Faithfulness metric,
(S52) which measures whether the generated answer is factually grounded in the retrieved documents.
(S53) Based on 100 test questions, the Scb10x-typhoon2.1-gemma3 model achieved the highest Faithfulness score of 0.1249,
(S54)outperforming the other models. This highlights its potential to generate trustworthy answers and effectively avoid hallucination, as demonstrated in the examples.
(S55)In conclusion, this research demonstrates that using an embedding model specifically tuned for Thai legal data,
(S56)combined with our proposed SLP technique, can significantly enhance the performance of a RAG system.
(S57)Furthermore, we found that Small Language Models (SLMs) can yield strong results for legal tasks, offering a resource-efficient alternative.
(S58)However, this study has its limitations, including the scope of law,
(S59)which is focused only on consumer protection, and the limited size of the dataset, which may affect the generalizability of the findings.
(S60)For future work, we plan to expand the system's scope to include other areas of law,
(S61)such as the Civil and Commercial Code or business law. We also intend to enlarge the dataset to be more diverse and comprehensive,
(S62)which will allow us to further test and improve the system's generalization capabilities.
